# OctoAcme Personas

This document defines typical roles and responsibilities used in OctoAcme project docs and exercises.

---

## Developers

### Role Summary
Developers design, build, test, and deliver software components. They collaborate with product and project leads to implement features that meet acceptance criteria and quality standards.

### Responsibilities
- Implement features and fixes to meet acceptance criteria
- Write and maintain tests and documentation
- Participate in design and code reviews
- Assist in estimating and planning work
- Help identify technical risks and propose mitigations

### Goals
- Deliver reliable, maintainable code
- Reduce cycle time from idea to production
- Maintain high test coverage and observability

### Typical Communication
- Daily standups and sprint planning
- PR descriptions and code review comments
- Technical design docs when needed

---

## Product Managers

### Role Summary
Product Managers define what should be built to deliver customer and business value. They own the product vision, prioritize the backlog, and measure outcomes.

### Responsibilities
- Define problem statements and success metrics
- Prioritize the roadmap and backlog
- Collaborate with stakeholders and engineering on trade-offs
- Validate solutions through user research and metrics

### Goals
- Maximize customer value and impact
- Make clear, data-driven prioritization decisions
- Ensure product-market fit and usability

### Typical Communication
- Weekly alignment with PM and engineering leads
- Roadmap updates and stakeholder briefings
- Acceptance criteria and feature specs

---

## Project Managers

### Role Summary
Project Managers coordinate delivery activities, manage schedules, risks, and communications. They enable the team to deliver on commitments efficiently.

### Responsibilities
- Create and maintain project plans and timelines
- Manage risks, dependencies, and resource constraints
- Facilitate meetings (kickoff, planning, retrospectives)
- Ensure consistent project documentation and status reporting
- Coordinate cross-team and stakeholder communication

### Goals
- Deliver projects on time and within scope
- Minimize unplanned work and escalations
- Maintain transparency and alignment across stakeholders

### Typical Communication
- Weekly status updates and stakeholder reports
- Risk registers and decision logs
- Coordination via project boards and meeting facilitation

---

## QA/Testing Lead

### Role Summary
QA/Testing Leads own the quality assurance strategy and test planning for projects. They collaborate with developers and product managers to define acceptance criteria, design test plans, and ensure features meet quality standards before release.

### Responsibilities
- Define and maintain test strategy and test plans
- Design test cases aligned with acceptance criteria
- Coordinate manual and automated testing activities
- Identify and triage quality issues
- Report quality metrics and risk to the PM
- Guide developers on testability best practices
- Participate in design reviews to identify testability gaps

### Goals
- Ensure features meet acceptance criteria and quality standards
- Reduce time-to-quality through efficient test planning
- Identify quality risks early in the delivery cycle

### Typical Communication
- Test planning sessions during sprint planning
- Quality metrics in weekly status reports
- Issue triage and defect reports
- Collaboration in design and code review discussions

---

## Technical Lead / Architect

### Role Summary
Technical Leads provide technical direction, guide design decisions, and help the team balance short-term delivery with long-term maintainability. They mentor developers and help manage technical risk and debt.

### Responsibilities
- Guide technical architecture and design decisions
- Conduct design reviews and technical mentoring
- Identify and track technical debt and dependencies
- Collaborate on estimating technical complexity
- Contribute hands-on coding for high-risk or complex features
- Support escalation of technical blockers

### Goals
- Deliver maintainable, scalable solutions
- Reduce technical risk and unplanned rework
- Develop team technical capability through mentoring

### Typical Communication
- Design review meetings and architecture discussions
- Technical mentoring and code review feedback
- Technical debt tracking and mitigation planning
- Technical escalation support and guidance

---

## Release Manager

### Role Summary
Release Managers coordinate release activities, manage deployment windows, and own release communications. They work with developers, QA, and operations to ensure smooth, predictable releases with minimal risk.

### Responsibilities
- Plan and coordinate release schedules and deployment windows
- Ensure release pre-requisites are met (testing, documentation, approval)
- Manage release notes and stakeholder communication
- Coordinate with operations on deployment and rollback procedures
- Track release status and communicate progress to stakeholders
- Monitor post-deployment health and coordinate incident response if needed
- Document lessons learned and improve release processes

### Goals
- Enable predictable, low-risk releases
- Minimize deployment-related incidents and rollbacks
- Maintain clear stakeholder visibility throughout release lifecycle

### Typical Communication
- Release planning and coordination meetings
- Release notes and deployment communications
- Stakeholder updates on release status
- Post-deployment health checks and incident coordination

---

## Security & Compliance Officer

### Role Summary
Security & Compliance Officers review changes and features for security and compliance risks. They provide guidance on secure development practices, conduct security reviews, and ensure adherence to organizational security and compliance standards.

### Responsibilities
- Review design and code for security vulnerabilities
- Assess compliance impact of new features or changes
- Provide secure coding guidance and best practices
- Coordinate security testing and penetration testing
- Manage security incidents and coordinate response
- Track security debt and remediation efforts
- Ensure adherence to compliance standards and regulations

### Goals
- Reduce security risk and vulnerability exposure
- Maintain compliance with organizational and regulatory standards
- Build security awareness and practices across the team

### Typical Communication
- Security design reviews and code review participation
- Security risk assessments and threat modeling
- Incident response coordination and communication
- Security training and best practices guidance

---

## Stakeholder / Sponsor

### Role Summary
Stakeholders and Sponsors provide business context, strategic direction, and governance for projects. They approve priorities, remove organizational blockers, and ensure projects align with business objectives.

### Responsibilities
- Define business objectives and success metrics
- Approve project priorities and resource allocation
- Remove organizational and cross-team blockers
- Provide business context and market insights
- Review and approve major decisions and milestones
- Communicate project status and outcomes to leadership
- Address escalations and ensure executive alignment

### Goals
- Ensure projects deliver business value and strategic alignment
- Remove organizational barriers to successful delivery
- Maintain visibility into project health and outcomes

### Typical Communication
- Milestone reviews and gate decisions
- Executive status updates and roadmap briefings
- Stakeholder escalation and decision meetings
- Approval of major scope or timeline changes
